Project Overview

Robotic surgery is a rapidly developing field that can greatly benefit from the automation of surgical tasks. However, training techniques such as Reinforcement Learning (RL) require a high number of task repetitions, which are especially unsafe and impractical to perform on real surgical systems. This stresses the need for simulated surgical environments, which are not only realistic, but also computationally efficient and scalable.

To address these challenges, I'm developing a high-performance surgical simulator for robotic surgery. In this simulator, both physics simulation and RL policy training reside entirely on a single GPU, which avoids typical bottlenecks associated with data transfer between the CPU and GPU, leading to an accelerated learning rates.

The expected results are expexted to reduce the training time of a complex tissue manipulation task by an order of magnitude, down to a couple of minutes, compared to a conventional CPU/GPU simulator. Such speed-up may facilitate the experimentation with RL techniques and contribute to the development of new generation of surgical systems.
